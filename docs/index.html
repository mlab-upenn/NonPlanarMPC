Nonplanar Model Predictive Control for Autonomous Vehicles with Recursive Sparse Gaussian Process Dynamics

Ahmad Amine, Kabir Puri, Viet-Anh Le, Rahul Mangharam

Department of Electrical & Systems Engineering, University of Pennsylvania

Paper Code

Abstract

We propose a nonplanar model predictive control (MPC) framework for autonomous vehicles operating on uneven terrain. To approximate complex 3D vehicle dynamics, we learn a residual Gaussian process (GP) on top of a nominal model, conditioned on local terrain geometry. A recursive sparse GP enables real-time adaptation to changing slopes, banks, and bumps, and the learned model is driven by a Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) controller. Validation in a custom Isaac Sim environment confirms high tracking accuracy across challenging 3D surfaces.

Approach

A nominal model, corrected online by terrain-aware learning

01

Geometry-aware residual dynamics

A single-track bicycle model captures planar motion; a residual sparse GP compensates for everything the planar model misses. The GP is conditioned on local surface roll and pitch, derived from the height-field normals.

02

Recursive online learning

The sparse GP is updated at every step with recursive least squares and a forgetting factor, so the model tracks time-varying terrain as the vehicle encounters it, rather than relying on a fixed offline network.

03

Sampling-based control (MPPI)

The learned dynamics are embedded in a receding-horizon MPC and solved with MPPI. Rollouts are sampled and propagated in parallel on the GPU, querying look-ahead terrain geometry along the full horizon.

Results

Tracking on three nonplanar tracks

We evaluate in Isaac Sim on three generated tracks — kidney, L-shaped, and oval — using the Leatherback / F1TENTH vehicle, and compare the recursive-GP model against a single-track baseline with no residual compensation.

Track statistics. The L-track and oval combine large elevation range with steep, high-curvature slopes.
